Jehovah Shalom

The peace that comes before the battle. — Judges 6 / Gideon

Listen on Spotify Listen on Apple Watch on YouTube

You keep begging God to end the battle so you can finally have peace. And God keeps saying — no, I'm giving you peace so you can walk into the battle.

— Peace first. Then the battle.

What This Episode Looks At

Gideon is hiding in a winepress — and you don't thresh wheat in a winepress. That one detail, read right past by almost everyone, tells you exactly how afraid he was. This is the whole idea behind Under the Scope: take one thing the text actually says, put it under the lens, and watch what you've been missing.

Into that fear, God does something backwards. Before the army, before the fleece, before the 300 — He gives Gideon a name for Himself: Jehovah Shalom, The Lord Is Peace. Not the peace that shows up once the fight is over. The Presence that carries you into it.

Part of the series: What God Calls Himself

Jehovah Shalom is one name in a walk through every name God calls Himself. Follow the show so you don't miss one — each name is a closer look at who He is.
